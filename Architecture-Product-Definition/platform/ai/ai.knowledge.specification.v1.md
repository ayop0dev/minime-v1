# AI Knowledge Specification V1

## Status

**Version:** V1

**Status:** Canonical

**Layer:** Platform

**Platform Service:** AI

---

# Purpose

This specification defines how the AI Platform recognizes, reuses, and evolves knowledge across the Minime platform.

Knowledge exists to reduce unnecessary reasoning.

The AI Platform should prefer using existing knowledge before generating new intelligence.

Knowledge is an architectural optimization.

It is not canonical Product Data.

---

# Architectural Philosophy

The AI Knowledge model follows seven principles.

## Knowledge Is Discovered

Knowledge is not invented.

It emerges from existing platform information.

The AI Platform discovers relationships that already exist.

---

## Knowledge Is Read-Only

Knowledge never changes:

* Product Data
* Events
* Storage
* Analytics

Knowledge is constructed by reading existing information.

It owns nothing.

---

## Knowledge Exists To Avoid Repeated Reasoning

The primary purpose of knowledge is reducing unnecessary AI inference.

If the platform already understands something with sufficient confidence, it should reuse that understanding.

Repeated reasoning over identical information is wasteful.

---

## User Decisions Create Strong Knowledge

The strongest knowledge source is an explicit user decision.

Examples:

* accepted suggestion
* rejected suggestion
* manual edit
* explicit preference

Human decisions have higher confidence than generated AI reasoning.

---

## Knowledge Evolves

Knowledge improves as additional evidence becomes available.

The platform should continuously refine understanding rather than repeatedly starting from zero.

---

## Knowledge Is Disposable

Knowledge is temporary.

When supporting information changes, knowledge may become outdated.

Outdated knowledge should be rebuilt rather than preserved indefinitely.

---

## Knowledge Is Independent

Knowledge belongs to the AI Platform.

It should remain independent from:

* language models
* prompt templates
* inference providers
* implementation technologies

---

# What Is Knowledge

Knowledge is reusable understanding constructed from trusted platform information.

Knowledge is neither:

* Product Data
* Event
* Analytics
* AI Suggestion

Instead, it represents what the platform currently understands.

---

# Knowledge Sources

Knowledge may be derived from:

## Product Data

Current canonical truth.

---

## Historical Events

How the current state evolved.

---

## User Decisions

Verified human preferences.

---

## Analytics

Observed behavior.

---

## Rendering

Presentation quality.

---

## Storage Metadata

Technical asset characteristics.

---

## Previous Knowledge

Previously established understanding that remains valid.

Knowledge builds upon existing knowledge whenever appropriate.

---

# Knowledge Hierarchy

Not every source contributes equally.

General confidence order:

```text
Explicit User Decision

↓

Canonical Product Data

↓

Historical Events

↓

Analytics

↓

Rendering

↓

Storage Metadata

↓

Previous AI Reasoning
```

Higher-confidence knowledge should always dominate lower-confidence knowledge.

---

# Knowledge Construction

Conceptually:

```text
Platform Information

↓

Read

↓

Understand

↓

Connect

↓

Knowledge

↓

Reuse
```

Reasoning happens only after reusable knowledge has been exhausted.

---

# Knowledge Reuse

Knowledge should be reused whenever:

* the task is equivalent
* the supporting information remains valid
* user intent has not changed

Reusing knowledge reduces:

* inference cost
* response latency
* repeated reasoning
* operational complexity

---

# Knowledge Freshness

Knowledge is valid only while its supporting information remains valid.

Examples of changes that may invalidate knowledge include:

* Product Data updates
* accepted user suggestions
* rejected recommendations
* new historical Events
* significant Analytics changes

Freshness depends on platform state rather than elapsed time.

---

# Knowledge Confidence

Every reusable knowledge item conceptually has confidence.

Confidence increases when:

* users repeatedly confirm behavior
* supporting evidence grows
* platform observations remain consistent

Confidence decreases when:

* users reject suggestions
* Product Data changes
* historical evidence becomes inconsistent

Confidence guides reuse.

It never replaces user authority.

---

# Knowledge Scope

Knowledge should remain narrowly scoped.

Examples:

```text
User prefers concise biographies.
```

Good reusable knowledge.

---

```text
User likes everything generated by AI.
```

Poor knowledge.

Too broad.

Not supported by evidence.

Knowledge should always be evidence-based.

---

# Knowledge vs Context

Context is temporary information collected for one task.

Knowledge is reusable understanding derived from many contexts.

Context answers:

```text
What information is available now?
```

Knowledge answers:

```text
What has the platform already learned?
```

---

# Knowledge vs Suggestions

Suggestions are outputs.

Knowledge is input.

Suggestions may disappear.

Knowledge may remain useful.

Accepted or rejected suggestions contribute to future knowledge.

The suggestion itself does not.

---

# Relationship with Data

Data owns truth.

Knowledge understands truth.

Knowledge never replaces canonical Data.

---

# Relationship with Events

Events describe historical facts.

Knowledge recognizes patterns across historical facts.

Knowledge never edits history.

---

# Relationship with Analytics

Analytics measures behavior.

Knowledge interprets repeated behavior.

Analytics provides evidence.

Knowledge provides reusable understanding.

---

# Relationship with Context

Context is constructed first.

Knowledge is retrieved next.

Reasoning occurs last.

The preferred sequence is:

```text
Read Context

↓

Reuse Knowledge

↓

Reason Only If Necessary
```

---

# Cost Optimization

Knowledge is the primary mechanism for minimizing AI consumption.

Preferred order:

```text
Existing User Decision

↓

Existing Knowledge

↓

Existing Context

↓

Deterministic Logic

↓

Small Model

↓

Large Model
```

Every reused knowledge item avoids unnecessary inference.

---

# Implementation Independence

This specification intentionally avoids defining:

* vector databases
* embeddings
* semantic search
* retrieval systems
* cache technologies
* knowledge graphs
* memory implementations

The architecture defines only the role of reusable knowledge.

Implementation remains flexible.

---

# Canonical Rules

The AI Knowledge model follows these rules.

1. Knowledge is discovered, not invented.
2. Knowledge is read-only.
3. Knowledge exists to reduce repeated reasoning.
4. User decisions create the strongest reusable knowledge.
5. Knowledge becomes stale when supporting information changes.
6. Knowledge should be reused before generating new intelligence.
7. Knowledge remains evidence-based.
8. Knowledge is independent from AI providers.
9. Knowledge optimizes cost through reuse.
10. The user remains the final authority.

---

# Canonical Principle

The AI Platform should accumulate understanding rather than repeatedly regenerate it.

Every verified user decision, every confirmed pattern, and every stable relationship increases the platform's reusable knowledge.

Intelligence becomes more efficient as knowledge grows.

The objective is not to perform more AI reasoning.

The objective is to need less AI reasoning over time.

Knowledge is therefore the AI Platform's most valuable optimization layer.

It allows Minime to deliver better intelligence with lower latency, lower cost, lower token consumption, and greater consistency while preserving the user's role as the single source of truth.
