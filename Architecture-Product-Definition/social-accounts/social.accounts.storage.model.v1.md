# Social Accounts Normalized Handoff Record Specification V1

**Status:** Approved
**Version:** V1
**Domain:** Social Accounts

---

# Purpose

This document defines the normalized social account handoff record produced by the Social Accounts domain.

The handoff record is the structured output of the Social Accounts processing pipeline.

It is produced after:

* collection
* normalization
* URL generation

---

# Architectural Position

The Social Accounts domain is a processing domain.

It collects user-provided social account identifiers, normalizes them, and generates canonical public URLs.

The Social Accounts domain does not maintain canonical storage.

Connected Accounts is the canonical storage location for user social link records.

After the Social Accounts domain completes processing, it delivers the handoff record to Connected Accounts. Connected Accounts creates the canonical Connected Account record and is thereafter the authoritative owner of that data.

The handoff record is:

* transient
* not independently owned
* not a source of truth
* not directly consumed by rendering

---

# Design Philosophy

The handoff record carries only what Minime owns.

It never carries information that belongs to external platforms.

Examples of external data that are intentionally excluded:

* profile names
* avatars
* followers
* following
* biographies
* verification badges
* engagement metrics

---

# Processing Pipeline

```text
User Input

↓

Normalization

↓

URL Generation

↓

Social Account Handoff Record

↓

Connected Accounts

↓

Connected Account Record (canonical storage)
```

The handoff record does not persist after Connected Accounts has accepted it.

---

# Social Account Handoff Record

Each handoff record represents one public social account being transferred to Connected Accounts for canonical storage.

```text
Social Account Handoff Record

├── account_id
├── platform
├── identifier
├── public_url
├── display_order
├── source_mode
├── created_at
└── updated_at
```

---

# Fields

## account_id

Reference to the owning Account.

All social account handoff records belong to exactly one Account.

This ownership reference is carried into the Connected Account record upon handoff.

Immutable.

---

## platform

The registered social platform.

Examples:

* Instagram
* TikTok
* X
* Facebook
* LinkedIn
* YouTube
* GitHub

Only supported platforms may be included in the handoff record.

Platform values must come from the Social Accounts Platform Registry.

---

## identifier

The normalized identifier produced by the normalization engine.

Examples:

```text
hmn
```

```text
john.dev
```

The identifier is stored without presentation prefixes.

Maps to the `username` field in the Connected Account record.

---

## public_url

The canonical public profile URL generated by the URL Generation engine.

Example:

```text
https://instagram.com/hmn
```

Maps to the `url` field in the Connected Account record.

---

## display_order

Defines the intended presentation order of social accounts.

Maps to the `sort_order` field in the Connected Account record.

---

## source_mode

Indicates how the record was created.

Possible values:

```text
Smart

Manual
```

This field is processing metadata internal to the Social Accounts domain.

It is not transferred to Connected Accounts.

It never changes user behavior.

---

## created_at

Creation timestamp of the handoff record.

Immutable.

---

## updated_at

Last modification timestamp.

---

# Field Mapping to Connected Accounts

When the Social Accounts domain delivers a handoff record to Connected Accounts, fields map as follows.

| Handoff Record Field | Connected Account Field | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| account_id | account_id | Carried forward — ownership reference |
| platform | platform | Carried forward |
| identifier | username | Same value; canonical field name in Connected Accounts |
| public_url | url | Same value; canonical field name in Connected Accounts |
| display_order | sort_order | Same value; canonical field name in Connected Accounts |
| source_mode | — | Not transferred — processing metadata only |

Connected Accounts assigns `connected_account_id` upon creation of the canonical record.

---

# What Is Not Carried in the Handoff Record

The handoff record intentionally excludes:

* display names
* avatars
* follower counts
* following counts
* platform IDs
* OAuth tokens
* refresh tokens
* access tokens
* profile metadata
* profile descriptions
* account existence state
* verification state
* discovery confidence
* visibility state (managed by Social Icons Block per-block)

---

# Record Independence

Each handoff record is completely independent.

Producing a handoff record for one platform must never affect another.

Example:

Producing an Instagram handoff record never affects:

* TikTok
* LinkedIn
* GitHub

---

# Cross-Domain Handoff Contract

## Producer

Social Accounts domain

## Consumer

Connected Accounts domain

## Ownership

The Social Accounts domain owns the processing pipeline and produces the handoff record.

Connected Accounts owns the canonical records after handoff.

## Write Authority

The Social Accounts domain delivers handoff records to Connected Accounts.

The Social Accounts domain does not write to Connected Accounts directly.

The Social Accounts domain does not modify Connected Account records after creation.

## Read Authority

The Social Accounts domain does not read from Connected Accounts.

## Handoff Trigger

Handoff occurs immediately after URL generation completes.

There is no draft state or intermediate persistence between handoff record production and Connected Account creation.

## Validation

The Social Accounts normalization engine validates format before producing the handoff record.

Records that fail normalization are rejected before the handoff record is produced.

Connected Accounts does not re-validate records received through Social Accounts Setup.

---

# Relationship with Other Domains

## Input Normalization

Produces the normalized identifier included in the handoff record.

---

## Platform Rules

Provide platform definitions used during normalization and URL generation.

---

## URL Generation

Produces the canonical public URL included in the handoff record.

---

## Connected Accounts

Receives the handoff record.

Creates the canonical Connected Account record.

Owns the canonical social link data after handoff.

Connected Accounts is the source of truth for stored social link records.

---

## Rendering

Rendering receives social link data from Connected Accounts.

Rendering does not read directly from Social Accounts.

Rendering never regenerates URLs.

---

## Analytics

Consumes click events.

Analytics never modifies social account handoff records or Connected Account records.

---

## OAuth

Not stored here.

OAuth belongs to a separate domain.

---

# Design Principles

## Produce Canonical Data

Only canonical, normalized values are included in the handoff record.

---

## Generate Final URLs

Public URLs are generated once and included in the handoff record.

Connected Accounts stores and preserves them.

Rendering never rebuilds URLs.

---

## Platform Independent

The handoff record format is identical for every supported platform.

---

## Minimal Ownership

Only data owned by Minime is carried in the handoff record.

External platform data is intentionally excluded.

---

## Immutable Origin

Every handoff record carries information about how the social account was originally collected.

This metadata remains within the Social Accounts processing context and is not transferred to Connected Accounts.

---

# Canonical Statement

The Social Accounts Normalized Handoff Record is the structured output of the Social Accounts processing pipeline.

It carries the canonical normalized identifier, generated public URL, ordering intent, and ownership reference from Social Accounts processing to Connected Accounts for canonical storage.

Connected Accounts is the canonical persistence layer for user social link data.

The Social Accounts domain does not maintain canonical storage.
